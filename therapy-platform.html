Grounding Technique: 5-4-3-2-1

5

Name 5 things you see

Look around and notice details.

4

Name 4 things you can touch

Feel textures around you.

3

Name 3 things you hear

Listen to sounds around you.

2

Name 2 things you smell

Notice any scents.

1

Name 1 thing you taste

Notice any flavors.

This exercise helps ground you in the present moment when experiencing anxiety or panic.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

1. Feet and Legs

Tense your feet for 5 seconds, then release.

2. Abdomen

Tighten your stomach muscles, then relax.

3. Chest and Arms

Clench your fists and tense your arms, then release.

4. Neck and Face

Tense your face muscles, then relax completely.

5. Full Body

Tense everything at once, then release for deep relaxation.

Repeat each group 2-3 times. Best done in a quiet, comfortable place.

When to Seek Emergency Help

  • • Thoughts of harming yourself or others
  • • Severe panic or anxiety attacks
  • • Substance overdose
  • • Severe allergic reactions
  • • Loss of consciousness

If experiencing any of these, call 911 or go to nearest ER.
